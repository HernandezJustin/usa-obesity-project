OBESITY

Overweight

  • 32.8% of adults 20 years and over are considered overweight according to the Center for Disease Control.
  • 18.4% of adolescents are overweight according to Heart.org

Obese

  • 37.9% of adults 20 years and over are considered obese according to the Center for Disease Control.
  • 20.6% of adolescents age 12-19 are obese according to the Center for Disease Control.
  • 17.4% of children age 6-11 are obese according to the Center for Disease Control.
  • 9.4% of children age 2-5 are obese according to the Center for Disease Control.

ISSUES

Health

Being overweight or obese makes you more susceptiible to the following illneses:

  • Heart disease and stroke.
  • High blood pressure.
  • Diabetes.
  • Some cancers.
  • Gallbladder disease and gallstones.
  • Osteoarthritis.
  • Gout.
  • Breathing problems, such as sleep apnea and asthma.

Societal

There are social consequences to being overweight or obese:

  • There is a weight stigma, which can be defined as negative attitudes that affect an individuals interpersonal interactions and activities in a detrimental way.

SOLUTIONS

Excercise

  • Aerobic activity:

    Get at least 150 minutes of moderate aerobic activity or 75 minutes of vigorous aerobic activity a week, or a combination of moderate and vigorous activity.

  • Strength training:

    Do strength training exercises for all major muscle groups at least two times a week. Aim to do a single set of each exercise, using a weight or resistance level heavy enough to tire your muscles after about 12 to 15 repetitions.
